Introduction

  • ROBUST Trial: 24 week RCT testing individual vs. social-network enhanced lifestyle interventions for weight loss among Black and Hispanic adults with obesity
  • Fitbit Community Group: Introduced at week 16 to support engagement and physical activity during weight-loss plateau phase
  • Purpose of Group:
    • Maintain motivation
    • Encourage accountability
    • Provide peer support before study closeout
  • Today’s Focus: Participant feedback from qualitative interviews on their experiences in the Fitbit community group

Intervention Timeline

Week 0: Baseline

Weeks 1–15: Health coaching + Nutrition Counseling

Week 16: Nutrition Counseling + Introduction to community group

Week 20: Added to the group

Week 24: Closeout and Interview

Motivations for Joining

1. Community and Social Connection
Desire for social interaction, belonging, and connection with others
P162: "I'd like staying in community... I can talk to people about how I'm feeling"
P340: "having a group where I can relate to or they're having the same goals"
P491: "Just to see like how like how others was doing"
P563: "To have other people with like minded goals... striving for similar things to be as healthy as they can"
2. Accountability and Motivation
Seeking external support to maintain commitment and drive behavior change
P162: "I can also get well motivation"
P340: "to have accountability"
P576: "sense of accountability... I took it a lot more seriously because of the accountability"

Motivations (Continued)

3. Comparison and Competition
Using others' progress as benchmark for self-assessment and motivation
P562: "without meaning to be competitive... that's, you know, the comparison in the good way"
P603: "Having like a little friendly competition... it's nice to like win"
P491: "if I didn't remember, I was in a group I went, oh, I see. They was doing some steps. Maybe I should go get some stuff some too"
4. Health Journey Continuation
Extending existing health and fitness efforts through group participation
P340: "to continue to my health journey"
P576: "I wanted to continue the relationship... because of the sense of accountability"
P603: "I have done it previously because I had had Fitbit before... that was kind of like successful"

Feedback for Group Improvement

1. Enhanced Communication & Notifications
P162: "I wasn't getting anything in my Gmail... Usually you get prompts... And I wasn't really getting the prompts"
2. Increased Group Size & Participation
P340: "More people. Yeah, more engagement"
P491: "Maybe like put it out to more people so they could join like everybody. That was in a program"
P603: "you're walking against strangers. So you don't really know what you're up against"
3. Personal Connection & Face-to-Face Interaction
P563: "having like a 30 minute virtual powwow... so that people can at least put a face to the names"
P562: "maybe once a month or every two weeks like to talk a little bit about what we are doing"
P562: "I don't know if any other person sleep close to here. We could meet and walk for example"
P603: "inviting people that you know... we could meet each other and kind of like see what we're up against"

Additional Findings & Outlier Perspectives

Challenge Structure & Incentives
Participant suggestion for improving challenge participation
P563: "if challenges are going to be made, an incentive should always be there... when they don't... have incentive... There's a lot less desire to try to do that challenge"
Technical Barriers
Individual circumstances affecting participation
P576: "since I have this Canadian SIM card... I can't see that... Even the Fitbit isn't working... properly on this phone"
P603: "the flight thing... it wasn't like calculating my flights properly, so I was like doing like 2 flights and it was like not registered"
Positive Group Characteristics
Aspects participants valued about current structure
P563: "I think the size is great, I don't think it should go any bigger... I think everybody's women. So I think that's also nice too... it being gender specific is nice"
Varied Engagement Patterns
Different levels of group interaction and participation
P562: "No, no... I was told... we wouldn't meet in person, we wouldn't meet in zoom... it wasn't that interactive"
P603: "I participated in the challenges I sent a couple of cheers. But it wasn't really much going on in there"

Recommendations for Future Implementation

1

Implement Regular Video Meetups

Monthly virtual sessions to build personal connections and community

2

Improve Notification Systems

Enhanced prompts and communication channels to maintain engagement

3

Add Challenge Incentives

Meaningful rewards to increase participation in group activities

4

Maintain Group Characteristics

Keep gender-specific, appropriately sized groups (current demographic worked well)

5

Address Technical Barriers

Robust technical support and troubleshooting for access issues

6

Enable Real-World Connections

Facilitate in-person meetups when geographically feasible

Conclusions

Community Groups Show Promise

Participants were motivated by social connection and accountability, indicating strong potential for community-based interventions in health behavior change.

Implementation Quality Matters

Technical functionality, group size, and meaningful interaction opportunities significantly impact participant experience and engagement.

Move Beyond Anonymous Tracking

Future community interventions should prioritize relationship-building and personal connections over purely metric-based interactions.

Individual Preferences Require Flexibility

From challenge incentives to communication methods, participants expressed diverse needs that suggest importance of customizable intervention components.
